How to Use This Dashboard

  • Switch between tabs to explore Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Quantum Computing analyses.
  • Choose an algorithm/technique from the dropdown to see relevant graphs and metrics.
  • Adjust parameters in the sidebar and click "Run Analysis" to update the results.

Explanations of Calculations & Visualizations

  • Confusion Matrix: Shows the number of correct/incorrect predictions for each class.
  • ROC Curve: Plots True Positive Rate vs. False Positive Rate for different thresholds.
  • Feature Importance: Shows which features are most influential in the model.
  • Training/Validation Curves: Show how accuracy/loss changes over epochs.
  • Quantum Circuit: Visualizes a quantum circuit with gates and qubits.
  • Bloch Sphere: Represents the state of a single qubit on a sphere.
  • Quantum Kernel: Measures similarity between quantum states.
  • VQC Output/Loss: Simulates a variational quantum classifier's output and training loss.
  • Quantum Feature Map: Shows how classical data is mapped to quantum states.
  • Quantum Ensemble: Simulates outputs from multiple quantum models.

Quantum Computing Primer

Quantum computing uses quantum bits (qubits) that can be in superpositions of 0 and 1. Quantum gates manipulate qubits, and measurements extract classical information. This dashboard simulates quantum circuits, kernels, classifiers, and more.
Quantum Circuit: A sequence of quantum gates applied to qubits.
Bloch Sphere: A geometric representation of a qubit's state.
Quantum Kernel: Measures similarity between quantum states.
VQC: A quantum model trained to classify data.
Feature Map: Encodes classical data into quantum states.
Ensemble: Combines outputs from multiple quantum models.
